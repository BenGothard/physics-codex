Title: Motion in One Dimension
Estimated Minutes: 18
Summary:
Start with position, displacement, and average velocity so later graph and acceleration work has a clean conceptual base.
Learning Objectives:
- Distinguish displacement from total distance traveled.
- Compute average velocity from a motion description.
- Explain why sign and reference direction matter in one-dimensional motion.
Key Concepts:
- Position and displacement
- Average velocity
- Reference frames and sign conventions
Examples:
- A shuttle cart moving east then back toward its start
- A runner finishing a lap at the starting line
Quick Recap:
Displacement tracks net change in position, not total path length, and average velocity uses displacement divided by elapsed time.
Equations:
- name: Displacement | expression: Delta x = x_f - x_i | meaning: Measures net change in position. | variables: x_f is final position and x_i is initial position.
- name: Average velocity | expression: v_avg = Delta x / Delta t | meaning: Connects displacement to elapsed time. | variables: Delta x is displacement and Delta t is elapsed time.
Worked Examples:
- title: Shuttle cart run | problem: A cart starts at 2 m, rolls to 14 m, and ends at 9 m after 7 s. | steps: Identify the initial and final positions. || Compute net displacement from final minus initial position. || Divide displacement by elapsed time to find average velocity. | answer: The displacement is 7 m and the average velocity is 1 m/s to the right.
- title: Runner on a short track | problem: A runner goes 30 m east and then 30 m west in 20 s. | steps: Notice that the final position matches the initial position. || Compute displacement instead of total distance. || Use zero displacement in the average velocity equation. | answer: The displacement is 0 m so the average velocity is 0 m/s.
Common Mistakes:
- mistake: Treating distance traveled and displacement as interchangeable. | correction: Distance is the full path length while displacement is only the net change between start and finish.
- mistake: Dropping direction from velocity statements. | correction: Velocity needs sign or direction because it depends on displacement, not just magnitude.
Checkpoints:
- prompt: If you walk 4 m east and 1 m west, what is your displacement. | prediction: Write your prediction before revealing the explanation. | reveal: The displacement is 3 m east because only the net change from start to finish matters.
- prompt: If a motion ends where it began, what must be true about the average velocity. | prediction: Use the displacement definition before opening the tutor note. | reveal: Average velocity must be zero because displacement is zero over the full interval.
