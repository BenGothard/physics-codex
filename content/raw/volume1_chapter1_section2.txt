Title: Velocity-Time Graphs
Estimated Minutes: 20
Summary:
Translate motion descriptions into velocity-time graphs and use slope or area cues without guessing from the picture alone.
Learning Objectives:
- Interpret the slope and shape of a velocity-time graph.
- Use area under a velocity-time graph to estimate displacement.
- Compare flat and sloped graph segments to physical motion stories.
Key Concepts:
- Slope on a velocity-time graph
- Area as displacement
- Piecewise motion intervals
Examples:
- A car cruising and then braking
- A sprinter accelerating before settling into a steady pace
Quick Recap:
On a velocity-time graph, slope speaks to acceleration while the signed area under the curve tracks displacement.
Equations:
- name: Slope on a velocity-time graph | expression: a = Delta v / Delta t | meaning: The graph slope tells how quickly velocity changes. | variables: Delta v is the change in velocity and Delta t is the elapsed time.
- name: Displacement from a rectangle | expression: Delta x = v * Delta t | meaning: The area under a constant-velocity interval equals displacement. | variables: v is velocity and Delta t is time spent at that velocity.
Worked Examples:
- title: Constant cruise segment | problem: A car moves at 12 m/s for 5 s on a flat graph segment. | steps: Recognize the flat segment as constant velocity. || Compute the area of the rectangle under the graph. || Attach units to the resulting displacement. | answer: The displacement on that interval is 60 m.
- title: Braking line segment | problem: Velocity drops from 16 m/s to 4 m/s over 6 s. | steps: Compute the slope as change in velocity over time. || Keep the sign because the velocity is decreasing. || Interpret the negative result as braking. | answer: The acceleration is -2 m/s squared.
Common Mistakes:
- mistake: Reading the height of a velocity-time graph as displacement. | correction: Graph height is velocity; displacement comes from the signed area under the graph.
- mistake: Forgetting that a downward slope means negative acceleration. | correction: Slope carries sign, so decreasing velocity over time produces negative acceleration.
Checkpoints:
- prompt: If the graph is flat above zero, what is the acceleration. | prediction: Decide from slope, not from area, before revealing. | reveal: The acceleration is zero because the slope of a horizontal line is zero.
- prompt: If the line crosses below the time axis, what changes about the area interpretation. | prediction: Think about sign before you reveal. | reveal: Area below the axis contributes negative displacement because the velocity is negative.
Interactive Lab:
