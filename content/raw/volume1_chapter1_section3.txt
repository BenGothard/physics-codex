Title: Constant Acceleration Models
Estimated Minutes: 22
Summary:
Use constant-acceleration relationships to connect changing velocity, elapsed time, and displacement in one coherent model.
Learning Objectives:
- Relate acceleration to changes in velocity over time.
- Use constant-acceleration equations with clear variable meaning.
- Check whether a numerical answer matches the direction of motion story.
Key Concepts:
- Constant acceleration
- Velocity change over time
- Kinematics equations as linked models
Examples:
- A skateboarder speeding up on a straight path
- A braking car losing speed steadily
Quick Recap:
Constant acceleration means the rate of change of velocity stays fixed, so the velocity-time graph is a straight line and algebraic models stay consistent.
Equations:
- name: Velocity update | expression: v_f = v_i + a t | meaning: Final velocity changes linearly when acceleration stays constant. | variables: v_i is initial velocity, a is acceleration, and t is elapsed time.
- name: Displacement update | expression: Delta x = v_i t + 1/2 a t^2 | meaning: Displacement combines the starting velocity contribution with the acceleration contribution. | variables: v_i is initial velocity, a is acceleration, and t is elapsed time.
Worked Examples:
- title: Board launch | problem: A board starts from rest and accelerates at 2.5 m/s squared for 4 s. | steps: Use the velocity update equation with v_i equal to zero. || Multiply acceleration by time to get the velocity change. || Check that the final velocity direction matches the positive acceleration. | answer: The final velocity is 10 m/s in the positive direction.
- title: Braking car | problem: A car moving at 20 m/s slows at -3 m/s squared for 4 s. | steps: Plug the known values into the velocity update equation. || Keep the negative acceleration sign through the calculation. || Confirm that the result is smaller than the initial speed. | answer: The final velocity is 8 m/s.
Common Mistakes:
- mistake: Treating acceleration as if it were the same thing as velocity. | correction: Acceleration describes how velocity changes, not how far or how fast something already moves.
- mistake: Dropping the sign on acceleration in braking problems. | correction: Negative acceleration must stay negative in the equation or the motion story will stop making sense.
Checkpoints:
- prompt: If acceleration is zero, what does the velocity update equation reduce to. | prediction: Rewrite the expression before revealing the note. | reveal: It reduces to v_f = v_i, which is exactly the constant-velocity case.
- prompt: If acceleration and velocity point in opposite directions, what happens to speed. | prediction: Decide conceptually before reading the tutor answer. | reveal: Speed decreases because acceleration opposes the current motion direction.
